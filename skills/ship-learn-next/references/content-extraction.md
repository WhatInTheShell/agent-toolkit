# Content Extraction by Source Type

## Extraction Decision Tree

```
Is the lesson a behavior change or a knowledge fact?
  → Behavior change: KEEP (it's actionable)
  → Knowledge fact only: DROP (user can't practice a fact)

Does the advice require reps to internalize?
  → Yes: Include in plan
  → No (one-time setup): Put in Resources section only

Can the user produce an artifact from this advice within 7 days?
  → Yes: Design a rep around it
  → No: Break it down further until they can
```

## YouTube Transcripts

**Extract**:
- Specific techniques with named steps (not vague principles)
- Case studies/examples the speaker used — these become Rep 1 templates
- Timestamps only when the user will need to re-reference mid-rep

**Drop**:
- Motivational framing, backstory, intros
- Theory without a corresponding practice
- Advice that requires tools/resources not yet available

**Trap**: Speakers often bury the actual technique inside a story. Extract the behavior, not the anecdote.

## Articles / Blog Posts

**Extract**:
- The "how to" sections verbatim — these map to action steps
- Any stated metrics ("they sent 50 emails and got 3 responses") — these calibrate Rep 1 success criteria
- The author's specific sequence, not just their conclusion

**Drop**:
- Statistical claims without a corresponding action
- Comparisons to other methods (irrelevant until Rep 3+)

## Course Notes

**Extract**:
- The smallest project from the course
- Which modules enable Rep 1 (ignore the rest until needed)
- Any exercises or drills — these are pre-designed reps

**Pattern**: Most courses are structured as 20% essential + 80% depth. Identify the 20% for Rep 1.

## Books / Long-Form

**Extract**:
- The author's core process (often in one chapter)
- Any frameworks with named steps
- Specific examples you can replicate

**Trap**: Books optimized for sales often front-load motivation and back-load the actual technique. Scan the index for "how to" chapters first.
